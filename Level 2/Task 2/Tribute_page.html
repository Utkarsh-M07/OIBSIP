Biography

Kobe Bean Bryant was born on August 23, 1978, in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. The son of former NBA player Joe "Jellybean" Bryant, Kobe grew up with basketball in his blood. After spending much of his childhood in Italy while his father played professionally in Europe, he returned to the United States and became one of the most decorated high school players in Pennsylvania history.

In 1996, at just 17 years old, Bryant was selected 13th overall in the NBA Draft by the Charlotte Hornets and immediately traded to the Los Angeles Lakers — a franchise he would define for the next two decades. Playing his entire 20-year career in the purple and gold, he won five NBA championships (2000, 2001, 2002, 2009, 2010), was named Finals MVP twice, and was the NBA's Most Valuable Player in 2008.

Beyond statistics, Kobe was defined by his unrelenting work ethic — a philosophy he called the "Mamba Mentality." He studied the game obsessively, trained before the sun rose, and pushed every teammate around him to be better. After retirement in 2016, he won an Academy Award for his animated short film Dear Basketball, proving his genius extended far beyond the hardwood.

On January 26, 2020, Bryant, his daughter Gianna, and seven others tragically lost their lives in a helicopter crash in Calabasas, California. The world mourned the loss of one of sport's greatest icons. In 2021, he was posthumously inducted into the Naismith Memorial Basketball Hall of Fame.
